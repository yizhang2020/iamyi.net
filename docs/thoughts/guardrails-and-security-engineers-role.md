# Guardrails and security engineer’s role

The security team and R&D/IT share a common goal: delivering a secure product to ensure business continuity and growth. Depending on their level of involvement in a project, a security engineer (SE) can assume one of four roles to ensure the product’s security.

| Role | Responsibility | Guardrails (influence on project delivery) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Observer** | Investigates security risks for current and future projects. **Deliverable:** security risk assessment report. | No direct influence on project delivery. The SE raises awareness and obtains acknowledgment from the project owner for all discovered risks. |
| **Adviser** | Conducts investigations, researches, and proposes practical and feasible solutions to mitigate risks. **Deliverable:** secure architecture design or change. | Minor influence on project delivery. The SE communicates with the project owner to fully understand and address security risks, providing alternative secure solutions and accepting the risks if the owner proceeds with the current plan. |
| **Partner** | Coordinates resources and assists in delivering a secure solution. **Deliverable:** implementation of a secured solution. | Moderate influence on project delivery. The SE frequently participates in project delivery to ensure initial success. |
| **Gatekeeper** | Monitors and provides technical support throughout the project to ensure complete security. **Deliverable:** a fully secured solution. | Acts as a co-owner of the project. The SE works closely with other stakeholders to ensure the project’s successful delivery. |

## Conclusion

The role of a security engineer in project development can vary significantly depending on the project’s needs and the level of security involvement required. From being an observer to acting as a gatekeeper, the SE’s responsibilities and influence can range from merely identifying risks to actively ensuring the successful delivery of a secure solution.

By clearly defining these roles, we can better align our efforts with project goals and ensure that security considerations are appropriately addressed at every stage. This structured approach not only mitigates risks but also fosters a collaborative environment where security and development teams work together towards a common objective.
